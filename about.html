About Us

Established in 2009, the Medical Devices Unit is part of the Department of Clinical Physics and Bioengineering (DCPB), one of the oldest and largest Medical Physics departments internationally. DCPB has over 320 scientist, engineers and technologists diffused throughout Greater Glasgow and Clyde Health Board providing services to 25 major hospitals, 10 specialist units and over 60 health centres. It provides regional services ranging from acute to community care covering over half of Scotland's population.

Why do we exist?

The NHS is uniquely positioned to support the deployment of innovative healthcare technologies, to satisfy unmet needs in the NHS and facilitate more affordable models of care. These technologies have an important role to play in future proofing the NHS against prevalent pressures such as an ageing population and an increase in 'life-style' related disease. Our practical support for device development, technology evaluation, and the application of device regulations has been tailored to address these challenges, accelerate the introduction of new technology into the NHS and provide new opportunities for industry.
